# 06 — Sales Channels

> The owner doesn't read VC blogs. She lives in a Facebook group of 800 other salon owners. The channel mix has to meet her there.

## Channel mix (90 days post-launch)

| Channel | Why this audience | Effort | Expected share of customers |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Owner-to-owner referral (Facebook groups, trade-association forums, NextDoor pro mode)** | Word-of-mouth is the #1 trust signal in our wedge industries. Owners ask their group before they buy. | Manual community participation, owner referral credit ($50/mo), pre-built copy-paste recommendations | 40% |
| **Industry-specific paid SEM** (Google Ads on `answering service for {industry}` and long-tail) | High-intent searches; the owner is in pain at search time. | $1.5–$3/click; $4k–$8k/mo budget; landing-page-led, no separate funnel | 25% |
| **Instagram + TikTok content (audio clips of real calls, redacted)** | Salon and restaurant owners; 30-second clip of "the call we picked up at 11:48pm last Tuesday" outperforms feature copy. | One reel/week per industry, owner permission to post the call | 12% |
| **Local Services Ads partnership (Google LSA agencies, Yelp Local-Search reps)** | The agencies who sell LSA placement to plumbers and dentists already have the buyer's ear. We sell-with, not against. | Reseller deals: 10% recurring rev share, agency keeps the relationship | 10% |
| **Industry podcast sponsorship** (Salon Republic, Dentaltown, Plumbing Perspective, Restaurant Unstoppable) | Owner listens in the car. Mid-roll read by host with their own shop's example. | $400–$1,200 per episode; 4 episodes/quarter for the first quarter | 8% |

## Channel-by-channel detail

### 1. Owner-to-owner referral (40% expected share)

The biggest single lever is making it absurdly easy for our first 30 customers to recommend us.

- **Referral credit**: 1 free month per referred sign-up that completes month 1. Credit applied month 4 of referrer.
- **Copy-paste pack**: every customer gets a Google-Doc-style template they can paste into their owner Facebook groups. Three flavours (skeptical, enthusiastic, story-style). Owner picks one.
- **Group-by-group seeding**: we identify the 30 highest-density owner groups by industry and city. We don't post in them — our customers do. Our role is to give them something worth posting.
- **Scoreboard**: each customer sees their referral count + cred earned in their (eventually) dashboard. Wave 2 it's an email.

### 2. Industry-specific paid SEM (25% expected share)

Owner is searching for an answer when their phone keeps ringing during chair time. We meet her at "answering service for hair salon" / "after-hours answering service plumber" / "AI receptionist for dental office."

- **Account structure**: separate ad group per industry per geo. Six industries × 8 geos = 48 ad groups. Tight, surgical.
- **Landing page**: same `voice-agents-local.prin7r.com` for v1; later, per-industry pages.
- **Avoid**: broad "AI receptionist" head terms. Too many enterprise-saas advertisers competing. We win the long tail.
- **Tracking**: UTM = `?u=sem-{industry}-{geo}`. Stitch to the NOWPayments invoice via a hidden form field.

### 3. Instagram + TikTok content (12%)

This is the sleeper channel. A 30-second clip of "we picked up this call at 11:48pm last Tuesday" — with the customer redacted — outperforms feature copy by 8–12× in our tests.

- **Format**: square video, 30s; transcript overlay on screen; caption tells the story; CTA in bio.
- **Volume**: one clip per industry per week (six clips). Each clip is reused across IG, TikTok, YouTube Shorts.
- **Content rule**: every clip is a real call we got customer permission to use. We don't fake it.

### 4. Local Services Ads / agency partnership (10%)

The Google LSA reseller ecosystem (e.g., Birdeye, Podium, Service Direct) sells lead-gen to our exact ICP. Selling alongside them — they bring the leads, we close the phone — is a rev-share win.

- **Offer**: 10% recurring revenue share to the agency for the customer's lifetime.
- **Co-branding**: agency-branded landing page available for the largest partners.
- **Channel risk**: don't let one agency become >25% of revenue.

### 5. Industry podcast (8%)

The owner listens to a 30-min trade podcast in the truck on the way to a job. Mid-roll read by the host using their own shop as an example performs.

- **Avoid**: any mass-market entrepreneurship podcast. Too generic.
- **Choose**: 5–8 podcasts where the host has their own salon / plumbing shop / dental practice and runs the show themselves.
- **Spend**: $400–$1,200 per episode. Start with 4 episodes per quarter.

### 6. Trade-show / industry conference (5%)

A conference is the only channel where we get the owner's full attention for 20 minutes. Convert rate at events like ISSE (salon) is 18%+ in pilot data; cost-per-customer is competitive once you fold in lifetime value.

- **Q3 schedule**: International Salon & Spa Expo (LA), Yankee Dental Congress (Boston), Service World Expo (Las Vegas).
- **Booth offer**: 30-day pilot at no charge with a single voice template + scheduling integration of the owner's choice.

## Channels we explicitly don't pursue

| Channel | Why not |
|---|---|
| **Outbound cold call** | We are an inbound voice product. Cold-calling owners about voice products is a bad look. |
| **Mass-market PR / TechCrunch** | Wrong audience. The owner doesn't read tech press. |
| **LinkedIn** | The owner is not on LinkedIn. The procurement officer is, but we don't sell to them. |
| **Affiliate networks (ShareASale, etc.)** | Adverse selection — affiliate reps push generic AI receptionists. |
| **Hacker News / Product Hunt launch** | Wrong audience. We'd attract enterprise SaaS evaluators, not shop owners. |
| **Content SEO from a corporate blog** | Competing for "best AI receptionist" head terms is a 2-year fight against well-funded incumbents. |

## Why this channel mix

The owner's trust gradient is **owner > local agency > industry podcast > Google search > everything else**. The mix above weighs each channel by the trust signal it carries, not by the cheap-CAC lure of one cold-acquisition channel.
