# 05 — Audience Profile

> Two personas, one ICP, one anti-persona table. Rollcall's audience is small (≤25 employees), local, owner-led, and on the owner's mobile after 6pm.

## ICP — the ideal customer profile

A US or English-speaking owner-operator running a single-location service business with these characteristics:

| Dimension | Range |
|---|---|
| **Industry** | Dentistry, plumbing/HVAC, salon (hair/nails/lash), restaurant, clinic (general practice / vet / chiro), general contractor |
| **Headcount** | 2–25 employees |
| **Locations** | 1–3 |
| **Daily inbound call volume** | 15–80 calls per shop per day |
| **Average call duration** | 60–180 seconds |
| **Existing scheduling software** | Square, Booksy, Vagaro, Dentrix, Eaglesoft, Tebra, Mindbody, Housecall Pro, Jobber, OpenTable, Resy, Toast, Tock |
| **Average revenue per shop** | $300k–$2.0M annually |
| **Phone responsibility** | Owner, owner's spouse, or one part-time front-desk |
| **Buying authority** | Owner. Single decision-maker. |
| **Buying budget** | Comfortable with $200–$700/month for a "phone tool" if it pays for itself |

The ICP is **not** big-box dental DSOs, multi-location restaurant groups, MSOs, or franchise chains. Those are Concierge-tier (custom). They route through `desk@prin7r.com` and we sell them a customised pilot.

## Anti-personas (we politely send them elsewhere)

| Anti-persona | Why we don't sell to them |
|---|---|
| **Big-box dental DSO chain** (50+ locations) | They need an enterprise sales process, custom DPAs, EHR-grade audit logs. Refer to Concierge tier or to a different vendor. |
| **Multi-state franchisor** (Great Clips / Sport Clips / etc.) | Channel sale through corporate; not our v1 motion. |
| **Outbound-sales SDR teams** | We don't do outbound. We refer them elsewhere (Goodcall, Dialpad). |
| **Lead-gen / call-center buyers** | Rollcall is for the owner, not for a procurement officer optimizing for cost-per-call. We're product-quality competitive, not bottom-price competitive. |
| **Crypto-only no-KYC operators** | Even though we accept stablecoin, we keep clean books with named buyers and invoiced services. |
| **"AI for AI's sake" technical buyers** | If you want raw API access to Vapi/Bland, you don't need us — go direct. |

## Geography

- **Primary launch markets**: US English-speaking metros, especially Sun Belt + secondary cities (Knoxville, Lawrence, Tampa, Tucson, Sacramento, Greensboro). Why secondary cities? Less owner-saturation from existing AI-receptionist sales reps; more trust in word-of-mouth.
- **Secondary launch markets** (Q4): UK + Canada English markets; same product.
- **Out of scope for v1**: non-English markets. Translation is a Wave 4+ project.

## Decision-maker map

For 92% of Wave 2 deals, the decision-maker is **the owner directly**. This is the dominant pattern in the wedge industries. The other 8%:

- The owner's spouse (handles the office) is the gatekeeper. Sell to her too.
- A practice manager (in dental practices ≥10 chairs) — different motion, longer cycle, refer to Concierge.

## What this means for the website

- The landing page is written for an owner reading on her phone between two clients on a Tuesday afternoon. Hero is one breath. CTAs are large. Text is plain.
- The Concierge tier is a clearly separate column with a `mailto:` so DSOs and chains route there without confusing the owner-direct path.
- We do not put a "schedule a demo" calendar on the page in v1. We put a `desk@prin7r.com` mailto. Owners will just email us. The calendar widget gets in the way.
